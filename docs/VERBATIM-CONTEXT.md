# Verbatim Context

## One-line positioning

Adversarial review for AI.

AI is prone to overconfidence, hollow flattery, and hallucination. Verbatim challenges AI work before users act on it.

## Origin

Verbatim came from a repeated behavior.

When Arun received an important response from ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or another model, he often did not trust the response enough to use it as-is.

The manual workflow was:

1. Take the AI response.
2. Send it to another model.
3. Ask the second model to critique it.
4. Identify what was missing, wrong, overstated, or under-examined.
5. Bring the critique back to the original model.
6. Use the debate cycle to improve the answer.

That loop became Verbatim.

## Product structure

Verbatim is not a generic AI wrapper.

It has four distinct jobs:

### 1. Library

Library preserves valuable AI outputs.

This is the highlighting layer.

Important distinction:

```text
We do not highlight what we distrust.
We highlight what we want to keep.
```

### 2. Debate

Debate pressure-tests a specific AI response or claim across models.

This is the current acquisition wedge.

It maps to the original behavior:
- “I don’t fully trust this.”
- “What is missing?”
- “What would another model challenge?”
- “How do I make this more reliable?”

### 3. Council

Council runs a parallel multi-model panel.

This is useful when the user wants multiple views at once rather than a single critique loop.

### 4. Insights

Insights tracks patterns over time.

This can show model behavior, disagreement patterns, and eventually which models are better for which jobs.

## Current wedge

The current growth test focuses on Debate.

Target audience:

```text
Independent and boutique consultants
```

Why this audience:

- They are findable through ad targeting.
- They can pay for productivity and quality tools.
- They face reputational risk when AI-generated work goes into client deliverables.
- They often lack a team member available to push back on their work.

## Consultant pain

The consultant does not care about “AI hallucinations” in the abstract.

The consultant cares about:

- sending a flawed client deliverable
- missing a critical assumption
- overstating a conclusion
- relying on a confident but incomplete AI answer
- looking careless in front of a client
- needing a second brain but not having a team available
